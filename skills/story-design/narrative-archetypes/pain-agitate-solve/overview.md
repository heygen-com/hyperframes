# Pain → Agitate → Solve (PAS)

## Core Logic

Define a problem the audience already feels → amplify the emotional weight until inaction becomes unbearable → introduce the product as the inevitable remedy. The product earns its entrance by arriving only after the viewer is primed to want it.

## Emotional Arc Pattern

```
Anxiety/Frustration ──▶ Peak Tension ──▶ Relief ──▶ Confidence ──▶ Empowerment
     (hook)            (agitation)     (reveal)    (demo)          (CTA)
```

## Typical Section Sequence

| Order | Type              | Role                                               | Approx % |
| ----- | ----------------- | -------------------------------------------------- | -------- |
| 1     | hook              | Establish the problem with data or scenario        | 5-15%    |
| 2     | pain_point        | Amplify: show emotional/financial cost             | 10-20%   |
| 3     | benefit_highlight | Tease the solution concept (not the product yet)   | 5-10%    |
| 4     | product_intro     | Reveal the product as the vehicle for the solution | 10-15%   |
| 5     | feature_showcase  | Demo the product solving the pain                  | 25-35%   |
| 6     | cta               | Inspire action                                     | 10-15%   |

## When to Use

- The audience already experiences the pain (B2B broken workflows, operational friction)
- You have strong data points (industry stats, cost figures) to quantify the problem
- The product is a direct remedy to a specific frustration
- You need to build urgency before the reveal

## When NOT to Use

- The product creates a new category nobody knows they need yet (→ use Future Pacing)
- The product's UI demo is the strongest selling point (→ use Demo Loop)
- The audience has no existing pain awareness

## Variants (Golden Samples)

Three distinct approaches to PAS — same underlying logic, different emotional levers:

**Membrain — Statistic-Driven PAS** (70s, B2B Sales SaaS)
Opens with "81% of companies struggle" — uses industry data to validate pain, sells the coaching *category* before the product. Slow build, delayed product reveal at 40% mark.
Key technique: Stat-Pain-Solution sandwich, mantra structure ("Elevate. See. Coach. Grow.")
</membrain>

**Alpha — Culture/Identity-Driven PAS** (60s, Web3 Browser Extension)
Opens with crypto chaos to validate FOMO overwhelm. Fast-paced opening mimics market chaos, then calms after product reveal. Includes an "insider joke" beat for cultural bonding.
Key technique: Chaos-to-Order pacing, in-group signaling, passive solution positioning.

**Madison — Character-Driven PAS** (113s, AI Marketing SaaS)
Follows a restaurant owner protagonist drowning in digital tasks. Uses visual metaphor (task bubbles) and financial shock (invoice counter). Literal "snap" transition resets chaos to calm.
Key technique: Overwhelm Cloud, Invoice Shock, Conversational Demo.
